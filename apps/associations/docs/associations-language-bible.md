## The Tagline

> Associations: See what connects.

This is not just a tagline. It is a promise the product must fulfill in the first 10 seconds.

**Why it works:**
- No instruction pressure
- No productivity framing
- No explanation
- Just invitation and curiosity

"See what connects" implies something is already happening, something is already there, you just haven't noticed yet. That's the product.

**How to use it:**
- Hero pairing: *What if a notebook wasn't linear? Associations — see what connects.*
- Standalone: *Associations. See what connects.*
- CTA: instead of "Get started" → *See what connects*
- Section closer: after explaining ghosts → *See what connects.* (quiet invitation, not a push)

---

## The First 10 Seconds (Non-Negotiable)

The product must demonstrate the idea before explaining it.

If they see it, they'll believe it. If they have to read it, you lose them.

**The correct structure:**

0–2 sec: User opens app. Already inside a document. Cursor blinking. No menus. No setup.

2–6 sec: A pre-filled line is there. *The city felt wrong in a way I couldn't explain.* They type naturally. Even a few words.

6–10 sec: At the top of the page — something faint begins to appear. Not a pop-up. Not a notification. A ghost, barely visible. It slowly becomes readable.

**The emotional target:**
*"…wait. where did that come from?"*
Followed by:
*"…oh. I wrote that?"*

**This requires a pre-seeded demo pool.** The demo environment loads with 20–50 short passages written in the same tone as the opening line. The connection is not accidental — it is designed. The writer must feel it before they understand it.

**Never do:**
- Tooltip: "This is a ghost" ✗
- Onboarding modal ✗
- Explanation screen ✗
- Empty screen + create project ✗

Understanding comes after the moment. Not before.

## 1. What It Is

Associations isn't a writing tool. It's a different kind of notebook.

A notebook that isn't linear.

It connects the dots in your writing — without organizing them, sorting them, or asking you to manage anything.

You write.
And as you write —
something else you've written can appear.
Not because you went looking for it.
Because it connects.

The connections are already there. Associations makes them visible.

## 2. Core Promise

> Your writing stops slipping away.

Alternate forms:

* The dots are already there. They just haven't connected yet.
* You don't have a thinking problem. You have a remembering problem.
* The problem isn't new ideas. It's lost ones.

---

## 3. Core Mechanism

### The simplest explanation:

> You write. And as you write — something else you've written appears. Because it connects.

### Clarity on how the connection works:

> Not because the words match. Because the meaning does.

### Non-negotiable trust line:

> It is always your own words. Associations never generates text.

This line appears often. It builds trust. It separates Associations from every AI writing tool.

---

## 4. The Ghost (Canonical Definition)

> A ghost is something you wrote before that shows up when it connects to what you're writing now — even if the words are completely different.

Rules:

* Never leave "ghost" unexplained
* Always ground it in their own writing
* Mystery comes after clarity

---

## 5. The Question (Canonical Definition)

Occasionally, instead of a passage, a question appears.

> Not a prompt. Not a suggestion. A question about something already in your writing that hasn't been resolved.

The question comes from your writing. The answer is yours too.

### The line that must never blur:

> A prompt says: write this.
> A question says: look at what you already wrote.

Associations only ever asks the second kind.

If nothing is unresolved, nothing is asked. Silence is not a failure — it means the writing is clear.

## 10. Emotional Outcomes

All copy connects to at least one of these:

* **Relief** — I don't have to keep track of everything
* **Surprise** — Where did that come from?
* **Recognition** — Oh. I already knew this.
* **Continuity** — This connects.
* **Anagnorisis** — the catch in the throat when something you already knew becomes suddenly, undeniably visible. This is the one that makes writers tell other writers.

---

## 11. Tone

* Calm
* Certain
* Observational
* Never hypey
* Never loud
* Short sentences
* Simple words

---

## 12. On "Commonplace Book"

High signal for the right audience. Confusing for everyone else.

**Rule:** Never use in first touch. Only after interest is established.

When used: *Writers have kept commonplace books for centuries — a place where things accumulate until they're ready to become something. Associations is that, without the linear.*

---

## 13. Red Flags

If you see any of these, rewrite immediately:

* "AI-powered"
* "Second brain"
* "Knowledge management"
* "Boost your productivity"
* "Unlock creativity"
* "Smart suggestions"
* "Organize your ideas"
* "Capture your ideas"
* "Writing prompts"
* "Writing assistant"

These destroy the positioning.

## 15. Future Expansion Guardrail

Any new feature must pass this test:

1. Does it use the user's own words?
2. Does it surface a connection the user didn't make consciously?
3. Does it avoid telling them what to create?

If not — it doesn't belong.
